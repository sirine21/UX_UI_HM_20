Government Website Redesign

Redesigning the header and footer navigation bar for a governamental agency of chaice. The US Department if the Treasury was the website to be redesigned.

The Problem

Navigating the Department of Treasury was causing confusion for users.

The Goal

Creating a logical header and footer navigation that flows easily when users visit the site, while still keeping the government UI branding feel.

Gathering Insights

We gave 5 users tasks to perform using the website. After they completed their tasks we discovered some pai points they incountered.

Tasks

Major Pain Points

.Data button is not clear

.Sections on navigation bar not made for the general public

.Utility buttons are not made to be action buttons

.Very long explanation/ texts when looking at utility buttons

.Users would have loved it if the button would be highlighted once selected to give peace of mind

.Clarity on all the text, hard to read, needs more visual design

.User would have preferred to have drop down is on the lef

Ideation and Design

Based on the user input we started redesigning the home page navigation bar. Users were confused in finding the information for the tasks they were asked to do. They kept on using the search bar to navigate the website. We created sticky notes with primary and secondary buttons and started regrouping them baked on what made sense to us and the user. This lead us to create site map that will be the stepping stone to our redesigned navigation bar.
